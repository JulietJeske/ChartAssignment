A Tale of Two States: The Effects of a Statewide Mask Mandate

November 15, 2020
by Juliet Jeske

The U.S. has left state governments to plan their own strategies on how to contain the spread of COVID-19. This patchwork approach has led to drastically different outcomes. In the case of two states with similarly sized populations the disparity is dramatic.

As of Aug. 13th

Vermont (pop. 623,989) - 2743 cases

Wyoming (pop. 578,759) - 21,341 cases

Vermont issued a statewide mask mandate on Aug. 1st. To date Wyoming only has a mask mandate in four of its 23 counties wih two of those mandates coming as late as early Nov.

Face masks aren't the only way to control the spread of COVID-19 but they are one major factor.

Linsey Marr, PhD, expert on aerosol transmissions said in July,

"There is increasing evidence through comparisons of what's happening in different countries and in different states, in terms of mask usage and mandates, that masks help slow transmission of COVID-19."
